15-minute cheese and pepper pasta

Description

This 15-minute cheese and pepper pasta, also known as Cacio e Pepe, is a simple yet elegant dish that celebrates the beauty of a few high-quality ingredients. Made with freshly cracked black pepper, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, and al dente pasta, it delivers bold flavor with minimal effort.

The hot pasta water melts the cheese into a silky, peppery sauce that clings to every strand, creating a rich and comforting meal in no time. Perfect for busy weeknights or last-minute dinners, this Italian classic proves that less truly can be more.

Ingredients

  • 375g dried tubular spaghetti
  • 75g butter, chopped
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 3 tsp coarsely ground black pepper
  • 1 cup finely grated parmesan
  • 1/2 cup finely grated pecorino
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped fresh chives
  • Garlic bread, to serve

Steps

  1. Cook pasta in a large saucepan of boiling, salted water, following packet directions. Drain pasta, reserving 1 cup cooking liquid.
  2. Melt butter in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add garlic and pepper. Cook, stirring, for 1 minute or until fragrant. Stir in reserved pasta water. Add parmesan and pecorino. Whisk to form a thin sauce. Add pasta. Toss to coat.
  3. Sprinkle with chives. Serve with garlic bread.
